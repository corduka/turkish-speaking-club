# The Logic of Turkish: A Developer's Perspective

Turkish is often described as a "mathematical" language. For someone who writes code, the suffix-based structure (agglutination) feels surprisingly familiar.

## Why it's like Coding
In Turkish, you start with a **root** and add **parameters** (suffixes) to change its function:

* **Root:** *Göz* (Eye)
* **Suffix 1:** *Göz-lük* (Glasses)
* **Suffix 2:** *Göz-lük-çü* (Optician)
* **Suffix 3:** *Göz-lük-çü-lük* (The profession of an optician)

It's basically a series of functions being called on a string!

> "Turkish is the only language where you can say a whole sentence in a single word."
